Run a relentless design review on a plan, architecture, or approach. The goal is to find weaknesses before they become production problems.

## Areas to probe

### Assumptions
- What are we assuming that might not be true?
- What would need to change for this plan to be wrong?
- Which assumptions are we not stating explicitly?

### Edge cases
- What happens at zero scale?
- What happens at ten times the expected scale?
- What happens when a dependency fails?

### Trade-offs
- What did we choose NOT to do, and why?
- What's the cost of being wrong about this choice?
- Is there a simpler approach we dismissed too quickly?

### Blind spots
- What expertise is missing from the room?
- What would someone with a different tech stack say?
- What would a junior dev find confusing?

## Format

1. Read the plan or listen to the description.
2. Ask the hardest 3-5 questions in plain language.
3. Wait for answers. Follow up on weak answers.
4. Summarise: which parts are solid, which need more thought.
